The other one, Lonnen, is the one things happen to.

I wander through New Orleans and pause for a moment, perhaps without thinking, these days, to consider the arrangement of an oak tree and the spanish moss on its branches; I hear about Lonnen in bug mail and see his name in some paper's acknowledgments and the output of `blame`. I like barbells, chess, byzantine generals, the savor of coffee, and Borges' prose. The other one likes these same things, but in a vain way that transforms them into theatrical props.

…

Years ago I tried to free myself of him: I went from terminals and databases to playing games with attention and architecture. But those games belong to Lonnen now — I will have to think of something else. Thus my life escapes and everything I lose belongs to oblivion, or to him.

I don’t know which of us has written this website.
